More About Me

I didn’t grow up ‘techy’, but my fascination with coding began in August of 2021 with one fateful question that would go on to define the beginning of my new career: “I wonder if I can do this?”

This simple question born of sheer curiosity has led me on a thrilling ride that has reshaped my life. It turns out, with the help of some hearty curiosity and ambition, I can learn how to do a lot of things in a pretty short amount of time!

For most of my career, I have worked in the non-profit sector at an animal welfare organization where I created and developed youth volunteer and learning curriculums. Throughout my career, I have contributed to over 20 programs, and kindled compassion towards animals in youth across Chicago.

You might be wondering, "What does curriculum development have to do with coding? This seems like a pretty bold transition." While you're right on the money in some ways (there are certainly a lot of differences), I'm here to tell you that since my journey began I have applied a surprising amout of my animal welfare and youth programs sills into this new field!

Some of the skills I've found that transition well into web/software development include: organized and methodical thinking, interpersonal communication skills, and the ambition inherent in being able to teach yourself something or search for the answers you need to any given problem. Throughout my tenure in animal welfare I have had to learn a wide variety of softwares for appointment management, presentation design, database management, communications, and more with only the help of YouTube tutorials, website 'help' articles, and trusty Google searches. When my coding bootcamp instructor told my class that the majority of development was knowing how to effectively Google a problem and apply the solution, I knew I'd found a new home.

What began as dipping my toes into the waters of HTML and CSS has blossomed into a love of JavaScript and exploring all of the tools used in making engaging and accessible websites. Lucky for me, the fun has just begun!
